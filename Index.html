Public Schools See Declining Rates

By: Ebony Prescod

December 15th, 2022

Since August, the New York City Public School system has enrolled about 6,100 children, who were bused from Texas to NYC.

Despite the influx of students from Latin America, the number of Latinx students actually decreased in 2021-2022.

In fact, enrollment actually decreased for students of all races, except for the multi-racial category.

This steady decrease could be, in part, of charter schools. During the Cov-id Pandemic of 2022, charter schools saw enrollment increase of 15,000 students.

However, since Donald Trump met with several charter school leaders and gave them his support, black and brown advocates of charter schools have rescinded their support. Some students left, and therefore the increase of charter school attendees only rose by one percent.

It seems like charter schools are not the reason why public school enrollment has decreased. Simply, many families have left New York. However, the DOE is facing a crisis on how to fund education for migrant students of varying English proficiency and learning levels. The comptroller's office recommends 34 million dollars be allocated to assist with transitioning these children.
